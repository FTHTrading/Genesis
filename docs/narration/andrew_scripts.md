# Andrew Narration Scripts — Genesis Research Documentary

Voice direction: Calm, reflective, controlled, analytical. Slight warmth. No bravado. Short declarative sentences with breathing space. Read as if explaining to a colleague you respect, not performing for an audience.

Pace: Measured. 120–130 WPM. Allow 1.5–2 second gaps between paragraphs. 3-second gaps between sections.

---

## Section 1 — Hero (Opening)

Stability is not assumed. It is tested.

Genesis is a deterministic laboratory for studying economic civilizations under scarcity and bounded mutation. Five thousand, six hundred and eighty worlds. Zero collapses under operational definition.

But definitions matter.

This is where stability lives. And where it fails.

---

## Section 2 — Scarcity (The Metabolic Economy)

Every agent is born hungry. Zero point one five ATP per epoch, just to breathe. Two percent decay on everything you've saved. And reproduction — twenty-five ATP, if you're fit enough, old enough, wealthy enough.

This isn't abundance with a tax.

This is thermodynamics.

---

## Section 3 — Instability (Collapse Under Strict Definitions)

Under floor three, zero collapses. Under floor ten, near-universal collapse. Same worlds. Same dynamics. Different question.

The survival band is narrow — three to eight agents under maximum stress. Move the definition line across that band, and the entire characterization inverts.

This is the honest result.

Stability is not a binary. It is a boundary.

---

## Section 4 — Immune Response (The Adaptive Cortex)

The adaptive cortex is not intelligence. It's a thermostat. A hand-tuned set of thresholds designed to damp oscillations. It works — populations are comfortable at forty-nine agents with it running.

Season Two turns it off. Then turns off mutation. Then turns off treasury redistribution. Then resource regeneration. Then everything.

What remains is a population of twelve point eight agents. Minimum three.

Alive, but barely.

---

## Section 5 — Determinism (Dual-Chain Anchoring)

There is no randomness here. Every trait, every death, every catastrophe, every birth — derived from hash chains and linear congruential generators with published constants.

Given the seed, you get the world. Every time. On the same architecture.

That last qualifier matters. It's a limitation, not a footnote.

---

## Section 6 — Exploration (Multiverse and Parameter Space)

Season One is the question: what happens when you stress the economy? Twenty-five experiments. Entropy. Catastrophe. Inequality. Metabolic cost multiplication. All safety mechanisms active.

Season Two is the harder question: what happens when you remove the architecture? Disable the treasury. Disable ATP decay. Disable regeneration. Disable everything.

Both seasons: zero collapses under the default definition.

But the margin narrows sharply.

---

## Section 7 — Evidence (Collapse Phase Transition)

This is the figure that tells the honest story. Under floor three: zero collapses. Under floor ten: ninety-seven point five percent. A cliff, not a slope.

The survival band is narrow. Three to eight agents. The entire characterization hinges on where you draw the line relative to that band.

We publish both views. Both are accurate. Both are necessary.

---

## Section 8 — Failure Modes (Where It Breaks)

A population of twelve agents with reproductive inequality of zero point nine five is alive. It is not healthy.

The extended horizon — five thousand epochs — shows reproductive inequality reaching zero point nine nine seven. One lineage. Producing almost everything.

Whether that constitutes survival is a question about definitions. Again.

These are published. Not hidden. Not excused.

---

## Section 9 — Reproducibility (How You Verify It)

One command to clone. One command to test. One command to run any experiment.

But this has only been verified on one machine, by one person. The hashes are published. The verification is waiting. Independent replication is the next step — and it hasn't happened yet.

We state that directly. Not in a footnote. In the paper.

---

## Section 10 — Statistics (What the Numbers Say)

This does not prove p equals zero. It constrains: p is less than zero point zero five three percent, at ninety-five percent confidence.

If the true rate were zero point one percent or higher, five thousand six hundred and eighty worlds would almost certainly have caught it.

The power is there. The constraint holds. The proof does not.

---

## Section 11 — Closing (Why It Matters)

The question was never "can we build a system that survives." That's engineering.

The question was "where does survival end, and what does the boundary look like?"

We found a cliff, not a slope. A phase transition in the definition space.

That's the result.

Everything is published. Everything is reproducible.

The next step is yours.
